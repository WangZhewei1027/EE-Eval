Big-Theta (Θ) Notation Visualization

Big-Theta notation (Θ) describes the tight asymptotic bound of an algorithm's running time or space usage. When we say:

f(n) = Θ(g(n)),

it means f(n) grows proportionally to g(n) within constant factors for sufficiently large n. Formally, there exist positive constants c1, c2, and n0 such that for all n ≥ n0:

c1 · g(n) ≤ f(n) ≤ c2 · g(n)

This means f(n) is bounded both above and below by g(n), up to constant multiples.

Example: f(n) = 3n + 5 and Big-Theta(n)

Consider the function f(n) = 3n + 5. We claim that f(n) = Θ(n). Let's visualize f(n) along with c1·n and c2·n for some constants to see the bounds.
