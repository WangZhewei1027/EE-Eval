How Congestion Control Works

Congestion control is a mechanism that prevents network overload by regulating the rate of data transmission. Key concepts:

  • Congestion Window: Limits how much data can be sent before receiving acknowledgments
  • Slow Start: Begins with small window and doubles it until congestion is detected
  • Congestion Avoidance: Grows window more conservatively after reaching threshold
  • Fast Retransmit: Quickly retransmits lost packets without waiting for timeout
  • Fast Recovery: Resumes normal operation quickly after packet loss

In this simulation, connections will automatically adjust their transmission rates when congestion is detected.
