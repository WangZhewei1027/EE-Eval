ACID Properties of Database Transactions

Simulated Bank Account Transfer

This demonstration simulates a simple banking transaction transferring money from Account A to Account B. It illustrates the ACID properties:

  • Atomicity: All steps succeed or none do.
  • Consistency: The data remains valid after the transaction.
  • Isolation: Transactions appear independent and isolated.
  • Durability: Once committed, changes persist.

Try the transfer buttons and watch the logs below.

Account Balances:
  • Account A: $1000
  • Account B: $500

Explanation of ACID Properties

  • Atomicity: Either the entire transfer completes (money debited and credited) or nothing happens if an error occurs.
  • Consistency: Account balances never become negative, and total money remains constant.
  • Isolation: Each transfer runs isolated — intermediate states aren't visible.
  • Durability: Changes are saved to localStorage, so data persists even if you reload the page.
