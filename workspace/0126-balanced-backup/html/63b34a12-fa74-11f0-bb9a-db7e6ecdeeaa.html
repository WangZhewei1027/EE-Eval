HTTPS Demo

HTTPS (HyperText Transfer Protocol Secure) is the secure version of HTTP — the protocol over which data is sent between your browser and the website you're connected to. The 'S' at the end of HTTPS stands for 'Secure' and it means all communications between your browser and the website are encrypted.

What happens during an HTTPS connection?

  • Encryption: All data sent and received is encrypted, preventing eavesdropping.
  • Authentication: The website presents a trusted certificate proving its identity.
  • Integrity: Data cannot be modified in transit without being detected.

This simulation will show a simplified HTTPS handshake process where your browser and a server establish a secure connection.
