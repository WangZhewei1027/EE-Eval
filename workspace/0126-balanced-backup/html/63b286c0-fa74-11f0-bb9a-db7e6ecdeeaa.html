Understanding P vs NP with a Simple Example

P vs NP is one of the most important open problems in theoretical computer science. Informally:

  • P is the class of problems which can be solved quickly (in polynomial time) by a computer.
  • NP is the class of problems where a given solution can be verified quickly.

The famous question: Is P equal to NP? In other words: can every problem whose solution can be quickly verified also be quickly solved?

Demo: Subset Sum Problem (NP) vs Brute Force Search (P?!)

The Subset Sum Problem asks: Given a list of numbers and a target sum, is there a subset of numbers that add up exactly to the target?

Verifying a candidate subset sums to the target is easy and fast (P). Finding such a subset quickly is believed to not be efficiently possible (NP-Complete). Here we demonstrate a brute force search that checks all subsets - which grows exponentially.

Note: This is a simplified demonstration of P vs NP concepts for educational purposes. The Subset Sum problem is NP-Complete, no known polynomial time algorithm exists.
