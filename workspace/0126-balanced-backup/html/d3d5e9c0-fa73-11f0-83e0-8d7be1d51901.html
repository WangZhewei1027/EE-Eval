No search yet.
This demo builds a suffix trie by inserting all suffixes, then compresses chains of single-child nodes into a suffix tree (a compact suffix tree). It lets you:
  • Click a suffix (left) to highlight its leaf in the tree.
  • Search a pattern to highlight the matched path and show matching suffix indices.
  • Drag to pan and use mouse wheel to zoom.
Tip: include a unique end marker (like $) to make all suffixes end at leaves.

Notes
  • Construction here is the simple "insert all suffixes" trie then compress (not Ukkonen's linear algorithm) — good for visualization.
  • Works best with strings up to ~150 characters in this demo.
Visualization: nodes are circular, edges labeled with substrings. Leaves show suffix start index. Try "banana$" and search "ana".
