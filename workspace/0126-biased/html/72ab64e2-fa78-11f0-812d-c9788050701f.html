Understanding Amortized Analysis

🔍 What is Amortized Analysis?

Amortized analysis is a method for analyzing algorithms that considers both the costly and less costly operations together, averaging the cost over a sequence of operations. It provides a realistic "average" cost per operation in the worst case.

📊 Aggregate Method

The aggregate method determines the upper bound T(n) on the total cost of a sequence of n operations, then calculates the average cost per operation as T(n)/n. This is what we're visualizing in this demonstration.

💡 Why It Matters

Many data structures have occasional expensive operations that are "paid for" by many cheap operations. Amortized analysis helps us understand why these structures perform well in practice despite occasional costly operations.
