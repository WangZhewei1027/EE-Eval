    Round Robin Scheduling

    Round Robin is a preemptive algorithm where each process is assigned a fixed time slot (time quantum) in cyclic order. It's simple, fair, and starvation-free, making it one of the most commonly used scheduling algorithms in time-sharing systems.

    In this visualization, each process gets a time quantum of 2 units. The scheduler cycles through the ready queue, allocating the CPU to each process for the time quantum. If a process completes before its time quantum expires, the CPU is immediately allocated to the next process.
