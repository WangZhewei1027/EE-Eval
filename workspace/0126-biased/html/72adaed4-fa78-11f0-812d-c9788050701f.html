Understanding Overfitting

Overfitting occurs when a machine learning model learns the training data too well, including its noise and outliers, resulting in poor performance on new, unseen data. It's like memorizing answers instead of understanding concepts.

The overfit model above perfectly passes through every training point, but its complex, wiggly shape suggests it's capturing random fluctuations rather than the true underlying pattern. In contrast, the well-generalized model captures the essential trend while remaining smooth and simple.

Techniques to prevent overfitting include using more training data, applying regularization, simplifying the model architecture, and employing cross-validation. The goal is to find the sweet spot between underfitting and overfitting.
