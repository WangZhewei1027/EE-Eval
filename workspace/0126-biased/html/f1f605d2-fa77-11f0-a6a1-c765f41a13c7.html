How the estimate is computed

Interpolation Search uses a value-proportional formula to estimate where the target might be within the current low–high interval. This often outperforms binary search for uniformly distributed data.

Formula
pos = low + ((target - A[low]) × (high - low)) ÷ (A[high] - A[low])
