P vs NP

P vs NP is a fundamental question in computer science that deals with the relationship between two types of problems: P problems and NP problems.

P problems are problems that can be solved in polynomial time, meaning that the number of steps required to solve the problem grows at most polynomially with the size of the input.

NP problems, on the other hand, are problems that can be solved in non-deterministic polynomial time, meaning that there is no known efficient algorithm for solving the problem. This means that if a problem is NP-hard, it is at least as hard as the hardest problems in P.

NP problems are often referred to as "hard problems" because they are difficult to solve, even with the best possible algorithms.
