Amortized Analysis

Amortized Analysis is a mathematical technique used to calculate the average cost of an investment over a period of time. It helps to determine the total cost of an investment, the total amount of interest earned, and the average cost per unit of time.

It is an important concept in finance and is used in various fields, including economics, accounting, and business.

What is Amortized Analysis?

Amortized analysis is a method of calculating the average cost of an investment over a period of time. It takes into account the initial cost of the investment and the interest earned on it over time.

It is often used to calculate the total cost of an investment, the total amount of interest earned, and the average cost per unit of time.

How is Amortized Analysis Calculated?

Amortized analysis is calculated by dividing the initial cost of the investment by the number of periods over which the investment is held.

The formula for amortized analysis is: Total Cost = Initial Cost / Number of Periods

Example of Amortized Analysis

Suppose you invest $1,000 in a savings account that earns an interest rate of 5% per year. After 5 years, the total interest earned is $500.

The total cost of the investment is the initial cost plus the interest earned, which is $1,500.

The average cost per unit of time is the total cost divided by the number of periods, which is $300 per year.

How to Apply Amortized Analysis

Amortized analysis is a useful tool for comparing the costs of different investments. It helps to determine which investment is the most cost-effective and which investment has the highest potential for returns.

It can also be used to calculate the present value of future cash flows, which is an important concept in finance.

Real-World Example

Suppose a company wants to invest in a new project that will cost $100,000. The company expects to earn an interest rate of 10% per year. How much interest will the company earn on the investment over the next 5 years?

The interest earned will be $10,000 per year, and the total interest earned over 5 years will be $50,000.
