P vs NP

The Most Important Problem in Computer Science

The P vs NP problem is a fundamental question in computer science that asks whether every problem whose solution can be quickly verified by a computer can also be quickly solved by a computer.

The Essence of P vs NP

P problems are those that can be solved in polynomial time by a deterministic Turing machine. Examples include sorting a list or finding the shortest path between two points.

NP problems are those for which a proposed solution can be verified in polynomial time, but finding the solution may be much harder. The traveling salesman problem is a classic NP problem.

The million-dollar question: Is P = NP? If true, it would mean that problems we currently think are hard to solve actually have efficient solutions we just haven't discovered yet.

Potential Implications

A proof that P = NP would have profound consequences, revolutionizing fields from cryptography to medicine to artificial intelligence. Many experts believe P ≠ NP, but no one has been able to prove it.
