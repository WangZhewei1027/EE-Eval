Random Forest

An ensemble learning method that operates by constructing multiple decision trees during training and outputting the class that is the mode of the classes or mean prediction of the individual trees.

How Random Forest Works

The Random Forest algorithm creates a "forest" of decision trees, each trained on random subsets of the data and features. This randomness helps make the model more robust than a single decision tree.

When making predictions, each tree in the forest votes on the outcome, and the most popular result becomes the model's prediction. For regression tasks, the average of all tree predictions is used.

The power of Random Forest comes from its ability to reduce overfitting (through feature and data sampling) while maintaining good predictive performance. It's widely used for both classification and regression tasks in machine learning.
