P vs NP

P vs NP is a famous problem in computer science that deals with the relationship between computational complexity and the difficulty of solving specific problems.

It is a question that has puzzled mathematicians and computer scientists for decades.

What is P vs NP?

P vs NP is a classification of computational problems based on their computational complexity.

P (Polynomial Time) problems are those that can be solved in time proportional to the size of the input, i.e., the number of elements in the input.

NP (Non-Polynomial Time) problems are those that cannot be solved in time proportional to the size of the input.
