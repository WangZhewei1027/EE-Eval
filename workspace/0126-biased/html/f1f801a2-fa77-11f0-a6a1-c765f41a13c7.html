OSI Model — Layered Architecture

Elegant, conceptual visualization of the seven-layer Open Systems Interconnection model. Observe how responsibilities separate and stack visually.

7
Application
Interface for user-facing services
User interaction & APIs
Hosts applications enabling network-enabled services (HTTP, SMTP, SSH). Responsible for resource access and presentation triggers.
6
Presentation
Data representation & encoding
Translation, encryption
Formats, encodes, compresses, encrypts/decrypts data for applications — ensuring semantic interoperability.
5
Session
Dialog control & synchronization
Connections & checkpoints
Manages sessions between applications: establishing, maintaining and terminating dialogues, with synchronization points.
4
Transport
Reliable transfer & segmentation
Flow control & reliability
Ensures error-free, ordered delivery (TCP/UDP); handles segmentation, reassembly and flow control between hosts.
3
Network
Routing & addressing
Path selection & internetworking
Determines how data is transferred between nodes via IP addressing and routing across networks.
2
Data Link
Framing & physical addressing
Reliable local transfer
Frames packets for local delivery, MAC addressing, error detection and sometimes correction on a single link.
1
Physical
Bits on the wire
Electrical & mechanical interface
Physical signaling: cables, connectors, voltages, bit-level transmission and media characteristics.
