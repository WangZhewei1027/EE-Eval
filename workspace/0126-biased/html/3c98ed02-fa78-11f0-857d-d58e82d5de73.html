Amortized Analysis

A smooth journey from occasional expense to elegant average cost

Amortized analysis is a powerful tool in algorithm analysis that helps us understand the average cost per operation over a worst-case sequence, rather than focusing on individual operation spikes. Instead of analyzing the worst-case cost for each single operation, it evaluates the overall cost divided evenly, smoothing out occasional expensive operations into small, manageable increments.

Consider a dynamic array that doubles in size when full:

  • Most insertions are cheap: just placing an item (O(1) time).
  • Occasionally, an insertion triggers resizing and copying all elements to a new array (O(n) time).
  • Amortized analysis averages out these occasional expensive operations over the total number of insertions, demonstrating that the amortized cost per insertion remains O(1).

This perspective ensures a tighter, realistic upper bound on the cost, reassuring us that expensive steps, although impactful individually, don't degrade average performance drastically.
