How HTTPS protects you

Encryption: Data is encrypted so eavesdroppers can't read it.
Authentication: The server proves its identity using a digital certificate issued by a trusted authority.
Integrity: Messages are protected from tampering. The TLS handshake negotiates keys and establishes the secure channel illustrated above.
