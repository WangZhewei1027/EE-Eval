NP-Completeness

NP-Completeness is a concept in computer science that refers to a problem or task that is too difficult or complex to be solved by a general-purpose algorithm, even with a computer. It is often used to describe problems that require a deep understanding of the underlying mathematics or a large amount of data.

Example: The traveling salesman problem is a classic NP-Completeness problem that involves finding the shortest possible tour that visits a set of cities and returns to the starting point.
