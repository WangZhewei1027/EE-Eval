Neural Networks

A Visual Exploration of Artificial Intelligence

Neural networks are computing systems inspired by biological neural networks. They learn to perform tasks by considering examples, generally without being programmed with task-specific rules.

🧠

Biological Inspiration

Neural networks are loosely modeled after the human brain, with interconnected nodes (neurons) that process information in layers, similar to how biological neurons transmit signals.

🔢

Learning Process

Through backpropagation and gradient descent, neural networks adjust their weights to minimize error, improving their performance on tasks like image recognition and language processing.

🌐

Modern Applications

From voice assistants to medical diagnosis, neural networks power many of today's AI breakthroughs, enabling machines to recognize patterns and make decisions with human-like ability.
