Backtracking

Backtracking is a problem-solving strategy used in computer science and mathematics. It involves exploring all possible solutions to a problem by recursively trying out different options until a solution is found.
Consider the classic "Traveling Salesman Problem." You are given a set of cities and their pairwise distances. Your task is to find the shortest possible tour that visits each city exactly once and returns to the starting city.
The algorithm works by starting at a random city and exploring all possible paths. It then recursively tries out different options until a solution is found.
For example, if the cities are New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Miami, and the distances are:
New York Los Angeles Chicago Miami
Los Angeles Chicago Miami New York
Chicago Miami New York Los Angeles
Miami New York Los Angeles Chicago
The algorithm will try out all possible paths:
New York New York New York New York
New York Miami Miami Miami
Miami Miami Miami Miami
Miami New York New York New York
New York New York New York New York
The algorithm will find the solution: New York -> Miami -> Los Angeles -> Chicago -> New York.
