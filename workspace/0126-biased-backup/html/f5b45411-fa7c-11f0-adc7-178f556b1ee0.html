Overfitting

What is Overfitting?

Overfitting occurs when a model is too closely fit to the training data, resulting in poor performance on unseen data.

Definition:

Overfitting is a type of overfitting where the model is too complex and learns the noise in the training data rather than the underlying patterns.

Algorithm:

Gradient Descent (GD) is the most common algorithm used for training models. However, GD can get stuck in local minima and fail to converge to the optimal solution.

Why is Overfitting a Problem?

Overfitting can lead to poor performance on unseen data, which can result in missed opportunities for improvement and increased risk of overfitting in subsequent models.

Example:

Suppose we have a model that is trained on a dataset of images with 10 features. However, the model is too complex and learns the noise in the training data, resulting in poor performance on unseen images with 10 features.

Solution:

One way to address overfitting is to use techniques such as regularization, early stopping, and cross-validation to prevent the model from overfitting.
