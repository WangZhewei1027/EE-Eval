What this visualization conveys

Observe how problems congregate: P problems are efficiently solvable; NP problems are efficiently verifiable. NP-Complete problems sit at the intersection of hardness and verifiability — they are the "hardest" in NP.

P

Problems solvable in polynomial time (efficient).

NP

Solutions verifiable in polynomial time given a certificate.

NP-Complete

Members of NP to which every problem in NP can be reduced in polynomial time.

Concise explanation

P are problems we can solve quickly (in polynomial time). NP are problems for which a proposed solution can be checked quickly.

NP-Complete problems are the "hard core" of NP: if any NP-Complete problem has a polynomial-time algorithm, then every problem in NP does (P = NP). The Cook–Levin theorem established the first NP-Complete problem (Boolean satisfiability).

Practical note: many real-world tasks reduce to NP-Complete problems, so we often use heuristics or approximation when exact polynomial-time solutions are unknown.
