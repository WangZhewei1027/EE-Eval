About AVL Trees

An AVL tree is a self-balancing binary search tree where the difference between heights of left and right subtrees (the balance factor) cannot be more than one for all nodes.

This visualization demonstrates how AVL trees maintain their balance through rotations when nodes are inserted. The balance factor for each node is displayed in the yellow circle above the node.

Notice how the tree automatically rebalances itself to maintain optimal height, ensuring O(log n) time complexity for search, insert, and delete operations.
