Big−Ω Notation

Asymptotic Lower Bound of Complexity

Ω

Big Omega (Ω) describes a function's growth lower bound. For large n, the function grows at least as fast as Ω(g(n)).

By ensuring T(n) ≥ c·g(n) for sufficiently large n, Big Omega notation provides a guaranteed *minimum* growth rate of an algorithm’s time or space complexity. It's fundamental for understanding the performance floor beneath an algorithm's behavior.
