About Binary Search Trees

A Binary Search Tree (BST) is a node-based binary tree data structure where each node has at most two children referred to as the left child and the right child. For each node, all elements in the left subtree are less than the node's value, and all elements in the right subtree are greater than the node's value.

This visualization demonstrates the fundamental properties of BSTs through an elegant animated representation. Click "Animate Search" to see a demonstration of how searching works in a BST.
