Big-Theta Notation Θ

 Θ(g(n)) = \{ f(n) : f(n) \text{ grows asymptotically as } g(n) \}

Big-Theta notation (Θ) characterizes an asymptotically tight bound on the growth rate of a function.
It means an algorithm’s running time grows at the exact same rate, both upper and lower bounded by the given function.

The function f(n) is bounded below and above by the functions c₁·g(n) and c₂·g(n) respectively, for sufficiently large n.
This visually expresses Big-Theta, showing that f(n) grows “tightly” within those bounds, defining the exact asymptotic rate.
