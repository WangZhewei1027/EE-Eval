Big-Omega Notation

Lower-bound growth: showing how f(n) grows at least as quickly as a multiple of g(n).

Big-Omega (Ω) is used to express a lower bound on the growth of a function. Formally:
There exist positive constants c and n0 such that for all n ≥ n0: f(n) ≥ c · g(n).
This visualization demonstrates the idea: find a constant factor c and a threshold n0 so that f dominates c·g beyond that threshold.
f(n) = n² + 10n
g(n) = n²
