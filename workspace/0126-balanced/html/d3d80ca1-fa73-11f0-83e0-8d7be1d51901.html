How this shows backtracking

Backtracking explores choices (which column to place a queen in a row), enforces constraints (no two queens share a column or diagonal), and when a dead end is reached it undoes the last placement and tries another alternative. The visualizer yields detailed steps: trying a cell, checking against existing queens, placing, and removing (backtracking).

Press "Step" to advance the algorithm. "Play" animates. "Run to Next Solution" continues until the next complete valid arrangement is found. "Find All Solutions" searches exhaustively.
