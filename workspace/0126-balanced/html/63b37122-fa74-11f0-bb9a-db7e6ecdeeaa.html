Congestion Control Demonstration

What is Congestion Control?
Congestion control is a network mechanism to prevent excessive data from overwhelming network devices (routers, switches), avoiding packet loss and delays. This demo shows how a sender's transmission rate adjusts based on network congestion.

Blue boxes (packets) move from Sender through the Router Queue (limited capacity) to Receiver.
When the queue fills (congestion happens), the sender reduces its sending rate ("Throttle"). When the queue empties, the sender increases the rate ("Additive Increase") to use available capacity efficiently.

This models TCP's congestion control principles: Slow Start, Congestion Avoidance, and reacting to packet loss/congestion.
