ACID Properties of Databases

1. Atomicity

Transactions are all-or-nothing. If part of a transaction fails, the entire transaction fails.

2. Consistency

Transactions bring the database from one valid state to another, maintaining integrity.

3. Isolation

Transactions are isolated from one another until they are committed, preventing interference.

4. Durability

Once a transaction is committed, the changes are permanent, even in the case of a system failure.
