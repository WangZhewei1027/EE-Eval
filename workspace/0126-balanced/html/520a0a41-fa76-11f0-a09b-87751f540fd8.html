Amortized Analysis

A type of financial analysis that estimates the cost of an investment over time, taking into account the time value of money and the risk of the investment.

Time Value of Money

Time value of money refers to the idea that money received today is worth more than money received in the future.

Amortized analysis takes into account the time value of money by allocating the cost of an investment over a specified period of time.

Risk of the Investment

Amortized analysis also takes into account the risk of the investment by allocating the expected return of the investment over a specified period of time.

For example, if an investment has a 10% expected return over 5 years, the amortized analysis would calculate the expected return over the 5-year period.

Example

Suppose we want to invest $10,000 in a savings account that earns an interest rate of 2% per year for 5 years.

The interest earned would be $200 (2% of $10,000) over 5 years.

Amortized analysis would calculate the interest earned over the 5-year period, taking into account the time value of money and the risk of the investment.

Output

The amortized analysis would output the expected interest earned over the 5-year period, which would be $10,000 (initial investment) + $200 (interest) = $10,200.

This is the expected value of the investment over the 5-year period.
