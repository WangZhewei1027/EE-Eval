PageRank

PageRank is a measure of the importance of pages in an organization's search engine rankings. It is calculated by assigning each page its ranking on the search engine's algorithm based on the number of visitors who have interacted with it.

Here are the basic steps to calculate PageRank:

  • In the beginning, all pages in the site are ranked equally.
  • The algorithm assigns a score to each page based on the amount of traffic it has received from other pages in the site.
  • The higher the score, the more important the page is for users and search engines alike.
  • The algorithm also takes into account the quality of the content on the page and how well it meets search engine guidelines.
  • The algorithm calculates a total PageRank score by adding up all the scores assigned to each page.
  • The higher the total PageRank score, the greater the importance of the page for users and search engines alike.

The PageRank score is then displayed on the website's homepage or other relevant pages.

By analyzing the PageRank score, organizations can better understand their website's ranking in search engine results and make informed decisions about how to improve their visibility and credibility online.
